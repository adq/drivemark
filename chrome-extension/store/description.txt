DriveMark — Bookmarks backed by Google Sheets

Keep a simple bookmark list that stays in sync across all your devices. DriveMark saves every bookmark to a Google Sheets spreadsheet in your own Google Drive, so there's no extra account to create and no server beyond Google's holding your data.

How it works
• Click the DriveMark icon on any page to save it as a bookmark.
• Each bookmark stores the URL, page title, a folder tag, notes, and an excerpt.
• Browse, search, and organise your bookmarks from the extension popup.

Multiple lists
• Switch between different spreadsheets to keep separate bookmark collections (work, personal, research, etc.).

Requirements
• A Google account (used to read and write the spreadsheet).
• The extension requests access only to spreadsheets it creates or you explicitly open — it cannot see your other Drive files.

Privacy
Your bookmarks live in your own Google Drive. DriveMark does not operate any server and does not collect or transmit your data anywhere else.

Permission justifications

identity: Used to call chrome.identity.launchWebAuthFlow() to handle Google OAuth.

storage: Used for chrome.storage.local to persist auth tokens, spreadsheet IDs, and cached sheet data.

activeTab: Used implicitly by chrome.scripting.executeScript() in extractPageMeta(). The activeTab permission grants temporary access to the current tab when the user clicks the extension's action button, which is needed for scripting.executeScript to inject into the page to extract data for the bookmark.

scripting: Used in chrome.scripting.executeScript() to extract page metadata like OG tags, descriptions, and cover images from the active tab to populate the saved bookmark fields.
